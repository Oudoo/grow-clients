Client: Heka Pharmacy (Heka Wellness Hub)

LIVE 60 Minutes
60:00
READY
Pre-Meeting Readiness
0/5
Internal Use Only An induction meeting with a pharmacy client is the perfect opportunity to dig past surface-level marketing and uncover the operational realities that drive their revenue. The goal is to understand their business from the inside out.
The Translation Matrix (PM Rules)
🚫 Don't say "CAC" or "Lead Gen"✅ Say "Cost to bring in a new regular patient"
🚫 Don't say "LTV"✅ Say "Patient lifetime value and chronic refill retention"
🚫 Don't say "CRM / ERP"✅ Say "Patient management and prescription tracking system"
🚫 Don't say "Marketing ROI"✅ Say "Measurable impact on foot traffic and prescription volume"
Objective

Anchor the conversation on revenue reality, not aspirations. You need to understand where money is actually being made and what is being left on the table before proposing anything.

🗣️ Ask

"What is your primary growth objective for the next 6 to 12 months — are you looking to increase overall foot traffic, boost high-margin OTC sales, or grow your prescription volume?"

🗣️ Ask

"Which products or services are currently your most profitable, and which are underperforming?" (e.g., skincare, supplements, chronic medication, or in-house consultations)

🗣️ Ask

"What is the lifetime value of a typical patient here, and how are you currently working to increase it?"

Objective

Build a precise picture of who walks through the door, who they are failing to attract, and where the experience breaks down. This feeds the targeting and positioning strategy.

🗣️ Ask

"Who makes up the core of your current customer base?" (e.g., elderly patients needing chronic refills, young professionals buying vitamins/skincare, neighborhood walk-ins)

🗣️ Ask

"Are there specific patient segments you want to attract but are currently missing?"

🗣️ Ask

"What are the most common complaints or pain points your patients express?" (e.g., wait times, medication unavailability, lack of delivery options)

Objective

Understand the current state of their backend systems and retention mechanics. This is vital for proposing data-driven marketing, loyalty programs, and automated patient engagement.

🗣️ Ask

"How are you currently capturing and managing patient data?" (Understanding if they have a CRM or integrated ERP system is vital for proposing data-driven marketing or loyalty programs)

🗣️ Ask

"What does your current process look like for prescription refills and deliveries?" (Are they leveraging WhatsApp Business automation, a dedicated app, or manual phone calls?)

🗣️ Ask

"Do you currently run any patient loyalty or rewards programs, and if so, how is the data tracked?"

Objective

Uncover their unique selling proposition and the gaps in their current marketing and digital presence. This informs both the messaging strategy and where to allocate effort first.

🗣️ Ask

"Why do your best patients choose you over the larger pharmacy chains or the competitor down the street?" (This uncovers their unique selling proposition)

🗣️ Ask

"What previous marketing efforts have you tried, and what was the ROI or general result?"

🗣️ Ask

"How do you currently handle your digital presence and local SEO?" (e.g., Google My Business reviews, social media engagement)

Objective

Close by establishing what is actually actionable given their internal capacity and budget. Define the immediate next step and set expectations for the rollout timeline.

Client: Base Training Club

LIVE 45 Minutes
45:00
READY
Pre-Meeting Readiness
0/5
Meeting Objective Secure alignment, establish authority, and extract data for the DMAIC Project roadmap. Frame everything around Capacity (16% to 70%) and Operational Friction (Lead Leaks).
The Translation Matrix (PM Rules)
🚫 Don't say "CAC" or "Lead Gen"✅ Frame as "Cost to fill one spot" and "Connecting athletes to training"
🚫 Don't say "Churn" or "Retention"✅ Say "Keeping members committed through the summer"
🚫 Don't say "CRM" or "Funnel"✅ Say "Member tracking system" and "Sign-up pathway"
Psychological Setup

Acknowledge their 80 members. Paint the picture of hitting the 500-member capacity. Set the stage that organic alone won't fill 420 empty spots.

🗣️ The Script

"You have a capacity of 500, but you are at 80. If we fast-forward 12 months and this facility is completely full, what does that look like? Are you opening a second branch, or maximizing profit here?"

Psychological Setup

Gently confront the manual DM protocol. Explain that if we drive traffic now, the manual system breaks. Introduce the 14-Day Quick Win (Automated DM Routing).

🗣️ The Script

"You mentioned in the audit that following up with leads is a headache. Walk me through exactly what happens when someone DMs you at 11:00 PM on a Friday asking for prices. Who answers it? What happens next?"

⚡ Objection Handler: "We just need more reach/followers"

"Reach isn't your problem right now; routing is. If we get you 10,000 views tomorrow and 50 people DM you, your current manual reply system will drop 40 of them. Followers don't pay rent; members do. We install the CRM tracking first, then we turn on the traffic."

⚡ Objection Handler: "We just use spreadsheets"

"Spreadsheets are fine for 80 members. They will physically break your staff at 300 members. Right now, your CAC is 'NA'. If you want to hit your 500 capacity, we have to know exactly how much it costs to buy a customer. We can't track ROI on a spreadsheet."

Psychological Setup

Address the "Summer Heat" churn. Present the strategy to pivot offers (e.g., Early AM / Late PM classes) to offset the Egyptian summer drop-off.

🗣️ The Script

"There are 50 commercial gyms in Giza with air conditioning. Why do your 80 members choose to sweat it out outside at Base Training Club instead of going to Gold's Gym?"

⚡ Objection Handler: "We lose people because of the heat"

"That's an environmental reality, but it's an operational excuse. We don't fight the heat; we pivot the offer. We will run targeted ads for 'After Dark' sessions or '6 AM Sunrise' blocks specifically targeting people avoiding the sun. We offset the churn mathematically."

Psychological Setup

Explain the need for a basic CRM to track acquisition cost. Ask the 5 operational questions to build their customized roadmap.

What is the exact price of a standard 1-month membership?
What is the exact number of new members they want in the next 90 days?
Who is their absolute best customer? (e.g., 25-35 y/o males, corporate workers)
Are they willing to allocate a minimum test budget (5,000-10,000 EGP/month) for Meta Ads?
Who is the specific staff member responsible for checking the CRM dashboard daily?
Psychological Setup

Confirm the 14-Day Quick Win and get verbal commitment on the CRM installation timeline.
